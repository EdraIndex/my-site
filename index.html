About EDRA

Most ESG tools give you a score.
EDRA gives you a result.

Over 2,000 entities reported to GRESB in 2024, representing $8.8 trillion in real estate assets. Most of them submitted without knowing whether their data would pass. Fund managers and asset owners lose certification cycles not because their assets are underperforming — but because their data is. Coverage gaps. Continuity issues. Missing documentation. Structural errors that a GRESB reviewer will flag and a submission system won't warn you about.

EDRA was built by a GRESB Certified Individual to solve that problem at the source. It scores every asset across three planes — asset context, data quality, and performance readiness — using the same thresholds and gating rules that govern actual certification decisions. Every failing metric is flagged with a codified reason code. Every fix is mapped to the specific threshold it unlocks.

The outcome: You go into your next submission cycle knowing exactly where you stand. No surprises. No failed qualifications. No cycles wasted.

Why EDRA

Performance is gated.
That's by design.

If your data doesn't pass integrity thresholds, EDRA won't show performance. This isn't a penalty — it's a disclosure of fact. Performance calculated on insufficient data cannot be trusted.

01

Truth-first scoring

NULL values are never treated as zero. Worst-case continuity defines asset continuity. Performance silence is preferred over inferred performance. Accuracy over convenience.

02

Real certification logic

Thresholds calibrated directly against GRESB, ENERGY STAR, and LEED requirements. Every score maps to a real-world certification outcome — not an estimate.

03

Full audit trail

Every override, correction, and score change logged with field name, original value, new value, reason, approver, and timestamp. Defensible at every level.
